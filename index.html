Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

These projects really helped introduce me to the ways in which we can create using technology. There were many different programs I got to use and learn about while working on these projects. I had to rely heavily on this technology and I had to work within the confines of the technology to replicate what I was trying to make. In some ways this is limiting, but in other ways I found it to be really successful in allowing me to do things that wouldn't be possible without the programs.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Aspects of design that we are learning about in this course were essential in making my projects aesthetically pleasing. I definitely tried to consider alignment and fonts heavily in these projects to create the desired outcome. I was especially able to understand the design principles when creating my logos and then when analyzing what makes "good design". Being able to look at magazine spreads through this different lens has helped me understand these principles even more.

3.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

Particularly in the UX research portion, I was able to consider consumers and how design is perceived by the viewers. Also in conducting a study, I was able to think about the ethics of UX testing and prioritizing users when creating design. In listening to users opinions and then when editing my work, I had to consider how accessible and user friendly my designs were. I had to keep in mind how the things I was creating would be perceived by others. I adjusted my work accordingly, making sure it was readable and overall an enjoyable experience.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

Workshops in class were key in the revision of my projects. Not only could my peers give me helpful feedback, but seeing their work was really inspiring. I think that seeing how impressive my classmate's work was, ended up encouraging me to take certain risks and try different things in my work.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

My logos, cartoon, and good design project all drastically changed since I submitted them the first time for workshop. I was able to take in feedback and adjust my work for this final version. This was helpful in many ways, and I always knew what had to be changed after a workshop. I was able to identify the strengths in my work, and the ways in which I could improve it.

6. Learn to present your work

This is definitely the area I have improved the most in. Bringing my work to class became less merve wracking every time, and now I feel comfortable presenting what I have done even if it is not complete or needs significant revision. I think especially presenting something longer like the UX testing presentation was something I was able to do effectively.

Album Cover

Event Flyer

Course flyer for a class taught by my client.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

I have had a lot of technical problems in creating my project, becasue the virtual computing lab is not always the easiest to use. So exploring these technologies and programs is really important in successfully creaing my designs. I also think with the client project, choosing a program was reall important to allow the client access to the design as well. I think the types of technologies we use can really impact how and what we create, so what I created in Canva was very different than in InDesign.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

The resume and the course flyer I had to think a lot about design principles. The most difficult and prevalent issues I had involved spacing and alignment. I had to pay a lot of attention to how to arrange the text in an attractive, and easy to read way. I think typography was also difficult because I had to find fonts that complimented each other well and weren't "too similar" or "too different". I thought these projects had more text and really forced me to think abou these design principles further.

3.Recognize and respond to ethical concerns.

Specifically with the course flyer, I had to really think about accessibility and inclusivity knowing that it would actually be sent out to students. I found that it was really essential to take on the perspective of other people and how they would respond to the design that I was making. Even though there is still a significant amount of text, I was sent even more text by my client, and it took a while of us considering what was the most important information so we could cut it down. I knew less text would be more inclusive as well as more engaging.

4. Engage with others in a workshop environment

Workshop was really helpful in my designs. I struggled with the spread and my resume, so seeing other people's work and how they did certain things was really valuable in how to approach the revision of my work. Having space to ask questions about other's designs as well as my own design was important having mostly unfinished work going into the workshops.

5. Practice revision within and across media forms

Revision was the most important thing for me in these projects. For most of these projects, my final project ended up being completely different than what I started with. Particularly, the design consultation project was especially dependent on my revisions. It took a lot of work and time to get to the final product. Working with a client definitely played a huge role in this, so it helped me understand how revision in design will practically work in the real world.

6. Learn to present your work

I found these projects to be a little bit more difficult for me, so I cam to workshop with much less done than I had expected. It was definitely difficult to navigate how to present work I wasn't necessarily proud of yet. I also think the album cover was really interesting in terms of presenting because I got to talk about some of my interests like music, so that definitely made the presentation of my work easier. I think overall, continuing to show my work to my peers has made it increasingly easier and helping me be more comfortable in my work.
